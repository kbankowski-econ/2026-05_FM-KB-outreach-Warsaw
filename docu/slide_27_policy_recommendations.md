# Global Policy Recommendations

- **Manage commodity and supply shocks carefully:** Governments should allow domestic energy prices to adjust to supply constraints and avoid broad discretionary fiscal interventions. Any support must be temporary, well-targeted to vulnerable households or viable firms, and carefully aligned with monetary policy to avoid prolonging inflation.

- **Implement forward-looking fiscal adjustments:** High global debt, rising interest costs, and geopolitical tensions have narrowed fiscal space, requiring well-designed adjustment plans to preserve stability and reduce the risk of disruptive market pressures.

- **Enhance fiscal transparency and communication:** Maintaining market credibility requires communicating fiscal realities and policy trade-offs clearly, as well as avoiding hidden deficits and vague promises, which helps build social consensus for reforms.

- **Strengthen debt management:** Governments must implement credible medium-term debt management strategies that are closely aligned with fiscal frameworks to effectively manage rollover risks and reduce borrowing costs.
