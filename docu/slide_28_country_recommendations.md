# Tailored Policy Recommendations by Country Group

- **US:** The necessary medium-term adjustment requires both revenue and expenditure measures, specifically broadening the tax base, reducing tax expenditures, and addressing long-term pressures stemming from Social Security and Medicare.

- **China:** Policy should feature continued near-term fiscal expansion to tackle deflationary pressures and boost consumption, followed by significant medium-term consolidation that includes tax/social security reforms, tackling local government debt overhang, and scaling back poorly targeted industrial policies.

- **Advanced Economies:** Advanced Economies can no longer rely on broad consolidation commitments; they require specific measures to rationalize current spending—like subsidies and public wage bills—while explicitly reprioritizing budgets to manage difficult trade-offs between defense, demographic pressures, and green/digital investments.

- **Emerging Markets:** Resilient Emerging Market Economies must protect institutional gains by adhering strictly to structural balance targets. Meanwhile, vulnerable EMs must regain market confidence by eliminating costly fuel subsidies, broadening tax bases, and reforming state-owned enterprises (SOEs) to limit government guarantees and contingent liabilities.

- **Low-Income Developing Countries:** With shrinking foreign aid constraining essential services, Low-Income Developing Countries must prioritize building domestic revenue mobilization capacity. Meaningful gains can be achieved through strengthening administrative functions, leveraging digitalization, and rationalizing tax expenditures.
